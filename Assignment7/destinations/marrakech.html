Marrakech, Morocco

Culture Budget

About Marrakech

Marrakech is a major city in the Kingdom of Morocco, known for its vibrant markets, gardens, palaces, and mosques. The medina, a densely packed, walled medieval city dating to the Berber Empire, is a UNESCO World Heritage site filled with maze-like alleys where thriving souks (marketplaces) sell traditional textiles, pottery, and jewelry.

Why Visit Marrakech

Marrakech offers a sensory overload of color, scent, and sound. From the bustling Jemaa el-Fnaa square to the serene Majorelle Garden, from historic palaces to luxurious riads, the city presents a fascinating blend of ancient traditions and modern luxury. The city's rich cultural heritage, exquisite cuisine, and affordable prices make it an attractive destination for travelers seeking an exotic experience.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Marrakech is during spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when temperatures are pleasantly warm rather than scorching hot. Winter (December to February) can be cool, especially at night, but still offers plenty of sunshine during the day.

Quick Facts

  • Language: Arabic, Berber, French
  • Currency: Moroccan Dirham (MAD)
  • Time Zone: Western European Time (WET)
  • Population: 1 million
  • Weather: Hot desert climate with very hot summers and mild winters
